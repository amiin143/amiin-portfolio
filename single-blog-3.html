How I Built My Portfolio Website

Amiin Qalib 03 JAN 2025

As a developer and freelancer, having a professional online portfolio is essential. It not only showcases your skills but also helps potential clients trust your work. In this post, I’ll walk you through how I designed, developed, and deployed my portfolio website—step by step.

"Your portfolio is your voice when you're not in the room."

I wanted my site to be clean, fast, responsive, and speak for my skills.

1. Planning the Structure: I started with a basic outline of what I wanted to include—Home, About Me, Skills, Projects, Blog, and Contact. I sketched a wireframe using Figma to visualize the layout before writing any code.

2. Writing the Code: I chose HTML, CSS (with Flexbox & Grid), and a little bit of JavaScript. I kept the code modular and clean. I also used icons from Font Awesome and Google Fonts to enhance visual appeal.

3. Projects Section: I showcased real work and personal projects like a Student Attendance System, a Freelance Task Manager, and of course, this portfolio. Each project includes a title, short description, tools used, and links to GitHub or live demo.

4. Responsiveness: I used media queries to ensure the site looks great on desktops, tablets, and phones. I tested the layout on multiple devices and browsers to make sure it’s fully responsive.

5. Hosting & Deployment: I used GitHub to manage my code and Netlify to host the site. It’s free, fast, and supports continuous deployment. Every time I push an update to the GitHub repository, Netlify updates the live site automatically.

6. SEO & Contact Form: I added meta tags, an open graph image, and submitted the site to Google Search Console. For the contact form, I used Formspree to handle submissions easily without backend setup.

Building my portfolio wasn’t just about showing my work—it was an opportunity to express who I am as a developer. If you're starting out, I highly recommend building your own site—it’s a powerful investment in your career.
